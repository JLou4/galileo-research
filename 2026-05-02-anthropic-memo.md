## 1. Company Overview

- **Founded:** 2021, by siblings Dario Amodei (CEO) and Daniela Amodei (President), along with ~10 other former senior OpenAI researchers/executives [1][2]
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Legal Structure:** Public Benefit Corporation (PBC) — not a standard C-corp. Dual mandate: shareholder returns AND responsible AI development [3]
- **Mission:** "The responsible development and maintenance of advanced AI for the long-term benefit of humanity" [1]
- **Headcount:** ~2,300 employees as of late 2025, more than double from months earlier (Observer, April 2026) [4]. Estimates vary: TrueUp reports ~3,000 as of May 2026 [5]. SaaStr characterized Anthropic as having "~5,000 employees" including contractors [6]. **Conservative estimate: 2,300-3,000 FTEs.**
- **Revenue per employee:** At $30B ARR and ~2,500 employees, that's ~$12M per employee — one of the highest in tech history (SaaStr) [6]

**Key context:** Dario was VP of Research at OpenAI; Daniela was VP of Safety & Policy. They departed over disagreements about OpenAI's commercialization speed and safety priorities [2]. Other co-founders include Tom Brown (GPT-3 lead architect), Sam McCandlish, Chris Olah, Jared Kaplan, and Ben Mann [1][2].

### Revenue Mix
- **~80% from enterprise/API** via pay-per-token pricing (Sacra, Oct 2025) [14]
- **300,000+ business customers** as of Oct 2025 [14]

- **Claude Code:** $2.5B ARR by Feb 2026, doubling since start of 2026 [14][17]

### Revenue Reporting Note (CRITICAL)
Anthropic reports revenue from cloud resellers (AWS, Google) on a **gross basis** — counting total end-customer spend as revenue and booking partner payouts as expenses. This **inflates top-line figures relative to net-reporting peers** (Sacra) [14]. The actual take-rate after cloud partner revenue share is lower than headline ARR suggests.

### Burn Rate & Profitability
- **2025 cash burn:** ~$5.2B on $9B ARR (~58% burn rate) [18]
- **2026 projected:** Cash burn dropping to ~one-third of revenue (Fortune, Nov 2025) [19]
- **2027 projected:** Burn rate down to ~9% of revenue [19]
- **Profitability:** Dario Amodei said "we could be profitable in 2026 if the revenue grows fast enough" (Dwarkesh Podcast, Feb 2026) [20]
- **2028 projection:** $70B revenue, positive cash flow (TechCrunch, Nov 2025) [21]
- **Comparison:** OpenAI projects $14B cash burn in 2026 and cumulative $115B through 2029 [21]

### Capital Efficiency & Margins
- Anthropic trains models at **4x less cost** than OpenAI (SaaStr) [6]
- Inference costs down **90% year-over-year** [6]
- **Gross margins on inference expanded from 38% to over 70%** in the same period (SemiAnalysis, May 2026) [35] — this is arguably the most consequential datapoint: not just growing fast, but growing more efficiently
- Claude is the **only frontier AI model available on all three major clouds**: AWS Bedrock, Google Cloud Vertex AI, and Microsoft Azure Foundry [35]

### Key Technical Differentiators

1. **Constitutional AI (CAI):** Anthropic's proprietary alignment technique — the model self-critiques against a set of principles rather than relying solely on human feedback [1]
2. **Hybrid Reasoning:** Instant responses when speed matters, multi-step thinking when accuracy is critical [14]
3. **Model Context Protocol (MCP):** Open standard for secure, real-time connections between Claude and enterprise systems. Reduces integration friction across AWS Bedrock and Google Vertex AI [14]
4. **Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP):** Tiered safety framework — each capability level triggers corresponding safety commitments. Unique in the industry [1]
5. **1M Token Context Window:** Generally available on 4.6 models at standard pricing (up from 200K) [14]

### Claude Code

- Available via CLI, VS Code, JetBrains, web, iOS, Slack [14]

- Anthropic committed **$100M in model usage credits** for Glasswing participants [25]
- Managed by new **Anthropic Labs** unit, co-led by Mike Krieger and Ben Mann [4]

## 8. Risks & Bear Case

### Competition Intensity
- **OpenAI** has 30GW compute target vs Anthropic's 7-8GW — a structural gap if compute remains the primary moat [29]
- **Google DeepMind** has essentially unlimited TPU access and deep integration with Google Cloud, Search, and Workspace — can distribute AI at unmatched scale
- **Meta** open-sources Llama, which commoditizes the model layer and could compress margins for API-only businesses
- **xAI** has Elon Musk's distribution (X/Twitter, Tesla) and a growing 100K+ GPU cluster

### Capital Requirements
- Despite raising $72.3B, Anthropic's infrastructure needs are accelerating. The Broadcom/Google deal commits to 3.5GW of compute capacity [9]
- Anthropic committed to **$50B in US computing infrastructure** and **$100B+ in AWS spending over the next decade** [8][9]
- If revenue growth slows before these commitments are fully ramped, the cash burn could become problematic
- **Revenue is reported gross** (includes cloud partner spend) — actual margins may be thinner than headline numbers suggest [14]

### Google Relationship / Concentration Risk
- Google is both a **major investor** and **primary compute provider** (TPUs via Google Cloud)
- Google also owns **DeepMind**, a direct competitor in the enterprise AI market
- If Google priorities shift (e.g., favoring Gemini distribution), Anthropic's compute access could be de-prioritized
- Amazon is attempting to lock in Anthropic with $33B in investment tied to $100B+ AWS spend commitments [8]
- **Dual-cloud dependency** (AWS + Google) creates negotiating leverage but also operational complexity

### Regulatory Landscape
- **Department of War dispute** (Feb-Mar 2026) is unprecedented: a US AI company designated as a "supply chain risk" by its own government [26][34]
- DoW threatened to invoke the Defense Production Act to force removal of safeguards [26]
- EU AI Act high-risk enforcement begins August 2026 — 7% global revenue fines for non-compliance
- **PBC + LTBT governance** may complicate IPO: public markets may assign a governance discount for the trust's escalating board control [33]

### Technical Risk (Model Commoditization)
- Inference costs down 90% YoY — revenue per token is declining even as volume grows [6]
- Open-source models (Llama, Mistral) increasingly capable — narrows the performance gap for most use cases
- If frontier capabilities become table stakes, the moat shifts to distribution (where OpenAI and Google have structural advantages)
- **Claude Mythos decision** — withholding the most powerful model from public release is a safety-first move but concedes first-mover advantage to competitors who release aggressively

### Anthropic-Specific Risks
- **Founder concentration:** Dario and Daniela Amodei hold outsized influence; LTBT governance adds complexity but not traditional investor protections
- **Enterprise revenue concentration:** 80% from API/enterprise. If a few large customers (Fortune 10) shift to in-house models or competitors, revenue could be volatile
- **Gross vs. net revenue:** The cloud reseller reporting method means actual economic margins are lower than they appear. Investors should model on estimated net revenue [14]
- **Safety stance as double-edged sword:** The Department of War dispute and Mythos withholding demonstrate Anthropic will sacrifice revenue for principles — admirable, but potentially costly if competitors don't face the same constraints
